Why Is The US Involved? Why Does It Matter?

Six connected reasons — each one leads directly to the next

1
Iran's nuclear program is months away from a bomb
Iran has enriched uranium to 60% purity. Weapons grade is 90%. US and Israeli intelligence both concluded Iran could reach 90% in under 3 months. Once Iran has a nuclear weapon, the entire calculus of the Middle East changes permanently — Israel can no longer consider military action, and Iran's "Axis of Resistance" becomes untouchable.

Israel cannot survive a nuclear Iran — it asked the US to act
Israel is a tiny country — 8,000 square miles, smaller than New Jersey. Iran's leadership has repeatedly called for Israel's destruction. A single nuclear weapon detonated over Tel Aviv would end the State of Israel. Netanyahu formally requested US air support, warning that Israel could not destroy Iran's underground facilities alone without American bunker-busting bombs.

The US has 40,000 troops in the Middle East it must protect
The US has 40,000+ military personnel stationed in Qatar, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Iraq, Kuwait, Jordan, and Syria. Since 2023, Iran-backed militias have attacked US bases over 170 times. After October 7, Iran activated Hezbollah, the Houthis and Iraqi militias simultaneously. The US decided it had to destroy the source of these attacks rather than play defence indefinitely.

4
Oil — the world runs on the Gulf, and Iran controls the tap
21% of the world's oil passes through the Strait of Hormuz every day. The US wants to ensure no single hostile power can switch off the global economy. Iran demonstrated in March 2012 and repeatedly since that it is willing to blockade the strait. India imports 60% of its oil through Hormuz. Japan, South Korea, and China are even more dependent. Allowing Iran nuclear weapons plus Hormuz control is unacceptable to every major economy.

5
Iran's "Axis of Resistance" — a network of armed groups across 5 countries
Iran funds and arms Hamas (Gaza), Hezbollah (Lebanon), PIJ — Palestinian Islamic Jihad (West Bank), Houthis (Yemen) and PMF — Popular Mobilisation Forces (Iraq). These groups collectively have more missiles than most European armies. Iran uses them as a buffer — so that any attack on Israel or the US triggers retaliation from multiple directions simultaneously, without Iran firing a single official Iranian shot.

6
Why India can't pick a side — and why it matters
India has $2.4 billion in active defence contracts with Israel, depends on Iran's Chabahar port for its Central Asia trade route, is a core Quad partner with the US, and has 5 million citizens in Gulf states that are now in a conflict zone. Taking any side loses at least one critical relationship permanently. India's only option is careful non-alignment — which is exactly what it is doing, at great diplomatic cost.
