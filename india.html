AI India Summary

India is caught at every pressure point in this conflict. Its oil supply, 8.9M diaspora in the Gulf, Chabahar investment, and carefully balanced diplomatic relationships with all combatants are simultaneously under stress.

For ordinary Indians, the most immediate impact is at the petrol pump. Crude above $97 means fuel price hikes are likely within weeks. Families in Kerala, Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu are watching with particular anxiety.

The government is managing quietly — working back channels intensively. Priority: protecting nationals, securing energy alternatives, and avoiding forced diplomatic choices between Washington, Tel Aviv and Tehran.

Mar 10
Day 1 · War BeginsChallengeEnergy
Brent crude spikes — India's $200B oil import bill threatened overnight
US strikes on Iran sent Brent surging past $84. Every $10/barrel rise costs India ~$15B/year and pushes fuel prices ₹5–6/litre at the pump. India's energy security ministry convened emergency calls with Gulf suppliers within hours of the first strike.
↓ India's Response
ResponseEnergy Security
Ministry of Petroleum ordered 30-day strategic reserve buildup. Fast-tracked crude deals with US, UAE and Iraqi suppliers. Indian refiners directed to activate emergency procurement protocols and identify non-Hormuz alternatives.
Mar 11
Day 2 · Diaspora AlertChallengeDiaspora
3.5M Indians in UAE on alert as conflict spreads to Gulf
With Iran striking UAE-proximate targets, India's largest overseas community faced immediate uncertainty. Gulf remittances — $125B/year — at risk. Kerala, Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu hit hardest as families flooded MEA helplines seeking news.
↓ India's Response
ResponseAviation · Advisory
MEA issued travel advisory urging Indians to avoid Iran, Israel and Lebanon. Air India and IndiGo began rerouting 1,200 affected passengers. DGCA granted temporary pilot duty relaxation to handle the surge in emergency travel demand.
Mar 12
Day 3 · Hormuz BlockadeChallengeEnergy · Trade
Hormuz blockaded — 60% of India's crude imports cut off for 4 hours
Over 60% of India's crude oil imports pass through the Strait of Hormuz. The 4-hour blockade triggered emergency calls with Gulf energy partners and panicked Indian refiners who had no alternative routing. Brent hit $94 — petrol pump hikes became inevitable.
↓ India's Response
ResponseDiplomacy · Trade
India used its unique relationship with Tehran to negotiate passage guarantees for Indian-flagged vessels. Quiet talks opened on releasing seized tankers in exchange for diplomatic goodwill — India's rare neutral position used as direct leverage.
Mar 13
Day 4 · Dubai StrikeChallengeSecurity · Diaspora
Drone hits Dubai airport — 200,000 Indians in direct blast radius
A Shahed drone detonated near Dubai International cargo terminal. Indian nationals make up the single largest expat group in the UAE. Flights halted, panic calls flooded MEA helplines, and the question of mass evacuation became real for the first time.
↓ India's Response
ResponseConsular · Evacuation
India deployed 3 emergency Air India flights to Dubai within 6 hours. MEA's consular crisis cell activated. Helpline set up — 4,200 Indians registered for priority evacuation. NDRF placed on standby for mass casualty protocol.
Mar 15–16
Days 5–6 · Chabahar at RiskChallengeTrade · Investment
US strikes near Chabahar — India's $85M port investment directly threatened
Strikes near Bandar Abbas disrupted Chabahar free zone operations — India's key strategic access route to Central Asia bypassing Pakistan. A decade of diplomatic work and $85M investment now paralysed overnight. Cargo diverted to Mundra.
↓ India's Response
ResponseDiplomacy
PM Modi called both Netanyahu and Khamenei — India's rare dual-channel diplomacy praised internationally. MEA reiterated: no war, protect civilians, resume dialogue. India refused to be forced into choosing sides between Washington, Tel Aviv and Tehran.
