Brent crude — war journey
Mar 10 baseline $76.20 · Key war events annotated
Brent Crude is the global benchmark oil price, set in the North Sea and used by India to price its imports. Every $10/barrel rise adds ~₹6/litre at the petrol pump and costs India roughly ₹1.5 lakh crore extra per year in import bills.
$10 rise = ₹6/litre more at pump

Brent vs WTI spread
$/bbl · war period · spread = Brent premium
WTI (West Texas Intermediate) is the US benchmark; Brent is what India pays. The gap (spread) shows how much more expensive India's oil imports are than the US price — war anxiety pushes Brent higher than WTI.

MCX Gold ₹ since war
₹/10g · safe-haven demand surge
MCX is India's Multi Commodity Exchange where gold is traded in rupees. In times of war, investors worldwide rush to gold as a "safe haven" — pushing prices up. Higher gold prices hurt jewellery buyers but benefit those holding gold as savings.
War = safe-haven demand surge

Oil vs Rupee — the correlation
As Brent rises, rupee weakens · dual axis · war period
India pays for oil in US dollars. When oil prices rise, India needs more dollars — increasing demand for dollars weakens the rupee (USD/INR goes up). A weaker rupee makes all imports costlier: electronics, medicines, edible oils — not just petrol.
Weaker rupee = costlier imports for everyone
